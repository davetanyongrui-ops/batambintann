About Batam

Indonesia's Most Dynamic Island Destination

Rising from the heart of the straits between Singapore and Sumatra, Batam Island spans 415 square kilometers as a premier Free Trade Zone of Indonesia. Since economic restructuring in the late 1980s, Batam has transformed from an industrial manufacturing base into one of Southeast Asia's most compelling leisure destinations — all within a short fast-ferry ride from Singapore's HarbourFront Centre.


The island's character is defined by its duality: in the central corridor around Nagoya and Batam Center, modern shophouses, five-star hotels, department stores, and business towers create a cosmopolitan energy that mirrors downtown Singapore but at roughly 40 percent of the cost. Eastward toward Tanjung Priok and Toko, the landscape shifts to green hills, traditional Malay fishing villages, and newly developed industrial estates.


What makes Batam genuinely unique is the breadth of its entertainment ecosystem. Southeast Asia's largest cable wakeboarding at Batam Wakepark, six championship golf courses with floodlit evening ranges, a food culture that stretches from Japanese precision kelong (floating restaurant) seafood to Indonesian satay under banyan trees -- this island rewards repeated visits because you literally cannot exhaust it in one trip.

Iconic Must-Visit Attractions

The Batam Bucket List

These are the experiences that turn first-time visitors into annual repeat travelers to Batam.

The Barelang Bridge (Jembatan Barelang)

Must-visit: The Barelang Bridge -- named after the composite of Batu, Raja, and Lang island -- consists of six suspended bridges connecting Batam's satellite islands. Modeled after Jakarta's Gelora Bung Karno complex, each bridge spans between 160m and 250m with striking cable-stayed geometry painted in white against the deep blue straits.


Visit at 5pm for optimal photography -- the setting sun creates dramatic silhouettes of the bridge towers while families and scooterists stream across in the evening commute. Park at the southern viewing platform on main Batam and walk along the coastal path that runs parallel to the waterway. Local warung stalls nearby serve coconut smoothies (es kelapa muda) and ikan bakar (grilled fish) for under SGD 5 per person.


 Best Time: 4:30PM-6PM |  Free Entry |  Photographer's Dream

Batam Wakepark & Ocarina Adventure Park

Southeast Asia's flagship: Batam Wakepark operates Southeast Asia's largest cable wakeboarding system with a 1,200m cable network that pulls riders at speeds up to 40 km/h across three lake courses designed for all skill levels. The park features a professional half-pipe ramp, slalom gates, and a beginner lagoon where instructors guide first-time riders through their opening session within 30 minutes.


Adjacent Ocarina Batam adds six zip-lines (the longest at 400m traversing between forested hills), a ropes course, aerial bridges, and the Skyline slide -- a series of wooden coasters that cascade down through the canopy. Group packages for 6-8 people range from SGD 35 to SGD 60 per person and include both wakeboarding and Ocarina access.


 Open 8AM-9PM |  From SGD 35/person |  Groups Welcome

Floating Kelong Seafood Experience

The Batam ritual: No visit is complete without a seafood kelong -- traditional wooden platforms built on stilts directly over the ocean, connected to shore by narrow bamboo bridges that sway gently as you cross. Kopral Jaya Kelong and Kelandit Island Food Park are two of Batam's most celebrated venues where freshly caught kingfish, mud crab, grouper, and squid are grilled or sour-brothed in front of you.


Book a sunset slot from 6PM onward. The entire table enjoys ocean views on three sides with waves audible beneath the platform, and the sky transitions through warm colors by the time you pay the bill (SGD 25-40 per person for an extraordinary meal). Live Malay singing often kicks up at 8PM -- bring your phone and prepare to be swept into a spontaneous karaoke session.


 Book 6PM Sunset |  SGD 25-40/person |  Live Karaoke Nights

Grand Batam Mall & Nagoya Hill Shopping

The FTZ advantage: Batam's Free Trade Zone status means imported goods -- electronics, cosmetics, leather goods, and designer brands -- arrive with minimal duties. Grand Batam Mall (with many outlets across four floors) anchors the Batam Center district alongside Nagoya Hill's basement retail strip where Singaporean shoppers regularly stock up during weekend trips.


Beyond shopping, Grand Batam houses a modern multi-screen cinema, international food court with Malaysian, Thai, Japanese, and Indonesian kitchens, arcade entertainment zone, and hypermarket section stocked with Singapore-brand products at reduced duty rates. Plan two to three hours comfortably.


 10AM-10PM Daily |  Free Entry |  Shoppers Paradise

Second-Trip Experiences

Batam for Return Visitors

You've done the bridge, the wakeboarding, and the mall. Now discover what locals treasure.

Pantai Barelang & Coastal Trails

White sand beaches meet forested coastal trails along Batam's southern shoreline. Pantai Barelang stretches across 600m of undeveloped coastline perfect for dawn walking, photography, or simply watching fishermen set their nets at first light. Connect the trail to Nagoya Hill's hilltop viewpoint for a panoramic俯瞰 of Batam Center's harbor and the Singaporean skyline visible on clear days 30km away.

Nongsa Peninsula Enclave

Batam's northeastern crown -- a lush 4,600-hectare peninsular reserve of mangrove forests, luxury villa compounds (Fairmont, InterContinental), surf breaks identified by international competitive bodies, and secluded beach access points unreachable without private transport. Rent a golf buggy from your resort for the 25-minute coastal drive through palm-lined roads.

Cultural Heritage Sites & Temples

Batam's cultural identity blends Malay, Javanese, Chinese, and Bugis influences across its temple landscape. The Hock Tek Kiong Temple (Batam Centre), Pure Land Temple on Gunung Malang hill, and the Monkey Forest near Lubuk Baja offer spiritual immersion complemented by panoramic island views. Visit for Morning prayers to see local rituals performed in traditional Malay-style chanting.
