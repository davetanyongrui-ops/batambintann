The Bintan Story

Where Luxury Meets Wilderness

Bintan is the Riau Islands' crown jewel — an island of 1,170 square kilometers where turquoise lagoons swallow entire shorelines, white sand beaches stretch for kilometers undisturbed by roads or development, ancient mangrove forests pulse with bioluminescent fireflies at dusk, and luxury resorts so exclusive their front gates double as private yacht berths.


Bintan's geography is extraordinary. The island contains within its boundaries ecosystems that exist nowhere else in the Indonesian archipelago -- golden sand dunes (Gurun Pasir) rolling across a tropical jungle interior, blue limestone karst caves where freshwater springs bubble through rock, mangrove river networks so wide their opposite banks vanish into mist.


Meanwhile, Bintan's Lagoi Bay development along the northeastern coast has become Southeast Asia's most ambitious luxury resort project. The Lagoi Bay Green City master plan encompasses five-star resorts (Ananda Bintan, Bintan Resorts), championship Jack Nicklaus golf courses, private beach estates, a recreational marina, and overwater villas built on stilts directly above crystal-clear shallow waters where the lagoon meets the open sea.


The contrast with Batam is intentional by design. Where Batam is about adrenaline, nightlife, shopping sprees, and urban energy, Bintan is about slowing down: sunrise yoga on a private beach, lunch at a resort that sources seafood from their own marine farm, firefly river kayaking at dusk that looks like stepping into an animated film.

Bintan Bucket-List Experiences

The Bintan Must-Dos

These are the moments that make Bintan a destination people travel internationally specifically to experience.

Treasure Bay & Chill Cove Lagoon

The engineered marvel: Treasure Bay is a 66-hectare man-made crystal-clear saltwater lagoon connected to the ocean by natural tidal flow -- creating water so clear and turquoise it looks like a tropical aquarium stretching across the entire shoreline. Chill Cove, adjacent at Bintan Resorts, extends this concept further with beach clubs, infinity-edge pools merging visually into the lagoon, and premium cabana rentals that come with dedicated butler service.


The shoreline features kilometers of white sand -- imported from Thailand during construction yet now covered in natural dune formations with native coastal vegetation re-establishing itself along the edges. The underwater visibility exceeds 8 meters, making it one of Indonesia's premier snorkeling spots accessible directly from resort property without any boat needed.


 Open Sunrise to Sunset |  Resort Guests Free / SGD 20 Visitors |  Snorkeling Paradise

Gurun Pasir & Blue Lake

The Sahara of Southeast Asia: Gurun Pasir (The Sands) sits in Bintan's deep interior -- a surreal landscape of golden sand dunes rising 10 to 30 meters above the surrounding jungle floor. At their center, perfectly positioned like nature's own jewel box, lies the Blue Lake (Danau Biru) with water so vividly blue it reflects clouds and sky in sharp detail while surrounded entirely by dense rainforest canopy.


The geological mystery of Gurun Pasir has puzzled scientists for decades -- how does a Saharan-style desert exist inside a tropical rainforest? The leading theory involves ancient sea floors uplifted by tectonic activity and sculpted by prevailing winds into the dune formations visible today. Hire a local guide from Bandar Bentan Telani (the main Bintan township) for the 30-minute drive plus guided trail walk through the dunes.


 Morning Visit Recommended |  Free + Guide SGD 15 |  Photography Spectacular

Mangrove & Firefly River Safari

The magical evening: This is arguably Bintan's single most unforgettable experience. The daytime component starts at Sebong Beach where you board a traditional wooden pontoon boat for a morning mangrove expedition through the Sebong River -- ancient mangrove tunnels with roots forming natural arches overhead, monitor lizards sunning on trunks, mudskippers hopping between tidal pools, hornbills calling from the canopy.


The evening component (starts approximately 6:30PM) sends you back to the same river under darkness -- and every branch within sight explodes into synchronized bioluminescence as thousands of fireflies (klipuk in Malay) light simultaneously, creating a living chandelier effect that has been compared to the legendary Rio Grande de Puno firefly tunnels. Book with a resort-arranged boat for SGD 30 per person which includes dinner by the river.


 AM Boat / PM Firefly |  SGD 30/person (w/dinner) |  Once-in-a-Lifetime

Bintan for Return Visitors

Deeper Bintan Experiences

Beyond the iconic spots, Bintan rewards repeat exploration through nature, heritage, and wellness.

Lagoi Bay Luxury District

Lagoi Bay is Bintan's answer to the Maldives -- a sprawling 4,600-hectare resort district along the northeastern coast featuring Ananda Bintan's private beach villas, Ayana's clifftop dining, and the Bintan Resorts complex with its adjacent golf course, beach club, and marine sports center. This is where Singapore's ultra high net worth individuals build second homes. Weekday rates at the budget-end resorts still start around SGD 180/night.

Bukit Besar & Mount Sahang Trek

Mount Sahang (840m elevation) is Bintan's highest peak and offers a challenging 3-to-4-hour round-trip jungle trek that rewards summiters with panoramic views across the South China Sea to Sumatra's volcanic coastline on clear days. Bukit Besar's secondary trails wind past seven natural waterfalls, limestone karst formations, and rare rafflesia flower sites. Requires moderate fitness and local guide (SGD 20). Visit dry season April through October.

Bintan resort Golf & Championship Spa

The Bintan Resorts golf course by John Harrington (Jack Nicklaus associates) delivers 27 holes across a stunning coastal ridge with ocean views from every par-3 hole and blind shots over native jungle valleys that demand precision navigation. Post-round, the award-winning spa offers traditional Indonesian jamu massages, banana-leaf body wraps, and hot stone therapy using locally sourced volcanic stones at rates starting SGD 45 -- an unbeatable value proposition for a world-class experience.
