The Golf Experience

Why the Riau Islands Are Golf's Best-Kept Secret

Six world-class courses span Batam and Bintan — from the oceanfront fairways of Palm Spring to the exclusive greens of Nongsa Country Club. Where palm trees line every tee, trade winds play through every valley.


Singapore golfers have long known that a short ferry ride unlocks extraordinary value: a round at a championship course in Batam or Bintan typically ranges from USD $50 to $85 — often on more interesting terrain with ocean views that Singapore's courses simply cannot replicate.


The Riau archipelago's golf portfolio features everything from championship par-72 layouts designed by international architects to innovative footgolf facilities where the whole group plays together. Palm Spring offers an all-inclusive resort golf experience (stay, play, dine on-site). Batam Hills challenges handicap players with dramatic elevation changes across its rolling hillside fairways. Tamarin splits your group between traditional golf and footgolf -- making it the ultimate friend-trip course.


Here is the Batam ritual that defines every golfer's visit: you play 18 holes ending around 3PM, hit the driving range for an hour as the sky transitions from white-hot to golden to deep blue, then walk into the clubhouse for cold drinks and Indonesian grilled seafood while discussing scorecards. That 4:30PM session at the drive tee with your golf crew -- that is when Batam's golf scene transforms from recreation to religion.

Six Championship Courses -- Detailed Guide

Every course described in detail with tee-time strategy, difficulty rating, and the experience that makes each one distinct.

01

Palm Spring Golf & Beach Resort

 Championship Par 72  |  6,800m length  |  Water Hazards on 14 of 18 holes

Location: Central Batam, near Nagoya Hill

Palm Spring is the Riau Islands' flagship golf-resort, combining an 18-hole championship course with a full-service resort complex including hotel rooms, beachfront restaurant, and conference facilities. The course was designed by a Thai-Burmese architectural firm known for tropical resort layouts.

Hole-by-hole character: The opening three holes are generous birdie hunters across flat parkland. Holes 4 through 7 introduce the first real test -- a dramatic sequence featuring the "Waterfall" par-3 (hole 5) where the green sits behind a cascading man-made waterfall with water splashing into the putting surface on overcast days. The back nine climbs uphill with holes 12 and 13 playing along elevated cliffs overlooking Batam Strait.

 Best for: All levels  |  Floodlit range available 5PM-9PM

02

Padang Golf Sukajadi

 Intermediate Par 72  |  6,500m length  |  Hilly terrain with natural drainage

Location: Sukajadi district, central Batam

Padang Golf Sukajadi is what every serious golfer secretly prefers over the flashier resorts: a course built by natural topography rather than bulldozers. Fairways follow old drainage patterns and seasonal streams, making approach shots strategic -- club up on certain holes where wind funnels through narrow valleys between hill crests.

The signature hole: Hole 14 at Padang is a par-3 that plays down an 80-foot slope to a green cradled between three ravines. Approach from the wrong club and you find yourself in knee-deep drainage -- every day of the week. The clubhouse restaurant is locally legendary for its nasi goreng special (SGD 5 with unlimited teh tarik).

 Best for: Scratch to Handicap 18  |  Floodlit driving range 4:30PM-9PM perfect sunset caddy sessions

03

Indah Puri Golf Club

 Championship Par 72  |  6,900m length  |  Coastal wind conditions

Location: Eastern coast, Batam Center approach

Indah Puri is the working golfer's favorite in Batam -- a 27-hole facility split into three distinct nines that can be combined in multiple configuration. The forward nine plays parkland (tropical gardens, mature banyan trees lining every fairway), while the back nine transitions to links-style holes along the coastal ridge where sea breezes create unpredictable lateral ball movement.

The strategy insight: Green speeds at Indah Puri run faster than any other course in Batam (Stimp 9-10) due to the elevated terrain and well-drained sandy soil. Many Singapore visitors over-club their wedges by one full club. Read the slope on putts from left to right -- the green complexes are subtle but true, with back-to-front tiers that drain ball direction aggressively.

 Best for: Advanced players who enjoy green reading  |  Evening range sessions with floodlights

04

Batam Hills Golf Resort TOP PICK

 Premium Par 72  |  7,100m length (back tees)  |  Elevation changes up to 120 feet

Location: Hilly interior, 40 min from Batam Center

Batam Hills is the archipelago's most prestigious golf challenge -- a course carved into rolling hillsides where every hole delivers a combination of elevation drama and strategic complexity not found on any other Riau Island facility. The design incorporates natural ridgelines, existing jungle preserves (the course retains 40% of its original tree canopy), and sweeping vistas across neighboring islands.

Hole-by-hole character: The signature sequence is holes 8 through 10 -- "The Ridge Run." Hole 8 (par-4) plays along an exposed cliff-top fairway with a drop-off to the sea on one side and dense jungle on the other, demanding a perfect position off the tee. Hole 9 (par-5) drops downhill over two tiers with water catching the green. Hole 10 pulls back uphill -- the hardest hole on the course at 468 yards with wind coming from three directions simultaneously.

 Best for: Competitive golfers, handicap 0-24  |  Sunset driving range at 4:30PM with views of Singapore across the strait

05

Tamarin Golf & Footgolf Club

 Dual Facility  |  Golf 18 holes + Footgolf 9 holes  |  Hybrid group fun

Location: Southern Batam, near Nagoya Hill

Tamarin uniquely splits your friend group between traditional golf and footgolf -- yes, kicking footballs into 21-inch holes on a golf course -- making it Batam's most socially flexible venue. For golfers: the 18-hole course is well-designed parkland with water features, wide fairways suitable for mid-handicap players, and a short-game area that includes chipping and putting practice green. For non-golfers: footgolf requires no experience, runs simultaneously on adjacent turf, and provides equal competitive engagement.

The afternoon strategy: Book a 4:30PM tee time. Play your round or footgolf session finishing around 6:30PM. The sun sets over the hills of southern Batam with spectacular red-violet skies. Stay for an hour at the floodlit driving range -- this is when everyone's swing loosens up, laughs replace scores, and friendships form or are tested.

 Best for: Mixed groups of golfers and non-golfers  |  Sunset 4:30PM tee-then-drive-range is the Batam classic day out

06

Batam Island Country Club (Nongsa)

 Exclusive Par 72  |  7,200m (longest in archipelago)  |  Members preference / resort guest access

Location: Nongsa Peninsula, northeastern Batam (Fortification of the Riau Islands)

Situated at the far northeastern tip of Batam where the coast bends into Malaysia's water, Nongsa Country Club is the archipelago's most exclusive course -- immaculate bunkers swept clean daily, well-maintained greens in championship condition (Stimp 11-12), and fairways mowed to 8mm. The location provides an unmatched golfing experience: holes that play along cliff edges with ocean vistas toward Malacca and the Karimata Strait.

The Nongsa advantage: Beyond golf, your location here puts you within minutes of Fairmont Batam villas, InterContinental resort complexes, and the private beaches that serve as the setting for Bintan's investment premium pricing (see our Contact page). Every golfer here knows: playing this course on land where property values exceed $35K/m2 changes how you view each hole -- you are literally playing across appreciation.

 Best for: Premium experience seekers, resort guests  |  Private sunset range access with caddie service from 4PM

The Batam Ritual

4:30PM Tee Time + Driving Range Under the Stars

This is the experience every Batam golfer remembers -- and returns for. Here is how it works:


The plan: Round up 4-6 friends after lunch or a morning swim at your resort. Each member grabs their club (or rents from the course's pro shop -- callus irons and wedges cost SGD $3/day). Drive to whichever course matches your group dynamic.


The timing: Tee off at 4:30PM. By the time you reach hole 6 or 7, the light has shifted from harsh white to warm gold -- every photo turns professional-level by instinct. Finish your round (or walk-off holes with friends) as the sky shifts from orange to deep purple through the evening.


The range session: This is where Batam's floodlit driving ranges become magic. Under bright stadium-style towers, you warm up your irons against each other: highest ball-speed challenge, closest-to-pin contest. The clubhouse serves cold Singha beer and ikan bakar until 11PM -- this is social golf at its absolute peak.


The best course for evening ranges: Palm Spring's range faces east toward Singapore across the dark strait with city lights twinkling on the far shore. Batam Hills' range sits 200 feet above sea level where you face three directions of ocean breeze -- testing every club in your bag.


 This is the single most repeated Batam activity among Singaporean golfers who visit quarterly. It is not just about golf -- it is about the ritual, the company, and the sunset that arrives at exactly the right moment.
